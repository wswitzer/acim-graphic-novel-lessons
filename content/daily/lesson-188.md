## Lesson digest

Lesson 188 moves from waiting for peace, light, or Heaven later into recognizing that the peace of God is already shining within now. The practice is to sit quietly, close the eyes, exclude the outer world, call wandering thoughts home, and let the light within restore perception. From that recognition, forgiveness naturally blesses all living things.

## Story synopsis

On a humid evening after the planting day, a sudden power outage drops Amara’s apartment courtyard into darkness. Neighbors call for her because she usually knows what to do, and she feels the familiar pressure to become the community’s emergency light. While searching for candles, she notices her mind racing into lists, worries, and responsibility. Instead of directing everyone, she sits for one quiet minute near the herb table, calls her thoughts back, and lets the peace already in her be enough. When she opens her eyes, the courtyard has not become magical or solved, but it has become gentle: phone lights, candles, mint tea, a crying child comforted, and neighbors helping one another.

## Character continuity

Amara completes a gentle three-lesson arc here. Lessons 186 and 187 showed her releasing heroic burden and the fear that giving would deplete her. Lesson 188 deepens that movement by letting her find present peace before acting, so care can extend through the community without becoming her control.
