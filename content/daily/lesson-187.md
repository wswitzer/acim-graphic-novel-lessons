## Lesson digest

Lesson 187 moves from the world’s belief that giving means loss into the recognition that a true thought is strengthened by being shared. Blessing must first be accepted within, and then offered outward without fear of sacrifice. The practical movement is to notice where a cherished form seems scarce, choose blessing instead of protection, and let shared care return in the form most helpful to everyone.

## Story synopsis

The morning after the community meeting, Amara brings a tray of young herb seedlings to a courtyard planting day. When more neighbors arrive than expected, she secretly wants to keep the strongest plants for her own windowsill, afraid that giving them away will leave her with nothing beautiful to tend. A child asks for the last healthy basil plant for his grandmother, and Amara notices the old idea of sacrifice tightening in her chest. She pauses, accepts blessing for herself first, and gives the plant gladly. By the end, neighbors have shared cuttings, recipes, water, and laughter; Amara walks home with fewer plants but a stronger sense that the thought of care has multiplied.

## Character continuity

Amara continues the new arc begun in Lesson 186. She has one lived example that her function is not carrying the whole community alone, but she still confuses giving with sacrifice. This story lets her practice a gentler version of the same healing: she shares one loved form and learns that the blessing behind it is not lost.
