## Lesson digest

Lesson 185 invites the student to move from merely saying “I want the peace of God” into meaning it sincerely. The practice is a careful, honest search of the mind for the dreams, bargains, compromises, and private outcomes still being asked for as comfort or happiness. The lesson’s movement is not shame about lingering illusions, but recognition that no private dream can give what God's peace gives, because real peace joins minds and cannot remain a private possession.

## Story synopsis

At the repair shop, Kenji prepares a careful estimate for Mrs. Sato's damaged music box and tells himself he wants peace. But his wish is mixed with a private bargain: if he stays exact, brief, and professional, he will not have to feel her grief. As he notices the bargain, he asks whether that distance is what he would have in place of peace. One simple sentence and one shared listening moment let the shop become quiet in a different way, not empty of feeling but joined.

## Character continuity

Kenji continues from Lesson 184, where he learned that labels and repair tickets can be useful without becoming anyone's reality. In this lesson, the next step is subtler: he notices that even his wish for quiet can become a bargain for separation. He does not become suddenly emotionally open or talkative, but he practices one small honest gesture that lets silence become shared intent.
